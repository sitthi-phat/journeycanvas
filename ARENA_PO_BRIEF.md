# Arena — PO Brief & Focus Guardrails

_Single source of truth for the Arena Product Owner role. Read this first to avoid losing focus._

## 1. Key objective (one line)
Launch **Arena** — a **new, standalone online English course platform** from Aksorn that delivers grade-appropriate English to Thai students (Grade 1–12) and **coaches them to international standards (CEFR · IELTS · TOEFL)** — sold **B2B-first** to schools.

## 2. What Arena is — and is NOT (do not conflate)
| | **Arena** | **Aksorn First** |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | **English only** | **All subjects** |
| Purpose | Online **English coaching** to a standard | Online learning **in the classroom** |
| Status | **New, standalone platform** | Separate existing platform |
| GTM | **B2B-first** (schools/teachers) | Classroom tool |

Arena is **not** part of Aksorn First and is not reached through it. Aksorn First is only cited as **credibility** (proof Aksorn already runs digital platforms) — always marked as a *separate* product.

## 3. Why it sells (business case)
- **Trust transfer:** Aksorn ~60% market share; 6 in 10 Thai schools already use Aksorn books/materials. A proven publisher launching a new platform is a low-risk bet for a school.
- **Proven digital capability:** Aksorn already runs Aksorn First + 2,000+ digital resources (e-books, Smart Presentations, 3D & AR).
- **Outcome-driven:** the promise is a measurable result — English skill benchmarked to CEFR/IELTS/TOEFL — which is what a school can show parents.
- **B2B value:** schools get a ready-made English coaching platform with **no need to build it in-house**.

## 4. Audience / persona
Primary: an **English teacher evaluating Arena for their school/class** (already an Aksorn user). Decision: "should we bring Arena to our school?"

## 8. Course segmentation — two candidate models (business view)
**Model A — Grade bands:** Grade 1–3 · 4–6 · 7–12 (focused course per band)
- + Maps to Thai school structure (Prathom/Matthayom) → drops straight into timetables & classes.
- + Aligns with Aksorn's existing grade-based curriculum/textbooks → reuses content & credibility.
- + Easiest B2B packaging — schools buy "the Grade 4–6 package"; intuitive for the buyer.
- − Grade ≠ skill level; a class will hold mixed abilities, and the CEFR outcome is skill-based.

**Model B — CEFR skill bands:** Beginner · Middle · Senior (A1–A2 / B1–B2 / C1+)
- + Directly aligned to the outcome promise (coach to CEFR/IELTS/TOEFL) → clearest, most measurable path.
- + Personalized by real ability → better outcomes → stronger ROI proof for the school.
- + Positions Arena as a **skill/coaching platform** (not a digital textbook) → justifies B2B premium & differentiates from Aksorn First.
- − Harder to slot into grade-based classes; needs a **placement assessment** up front (onboarding friction).
- − Less intuitive for a buyer who thinks in grades.

**PO recommendation:** the two are complementary, not either/or — **sell & organize by grade band (Model A), place & coach by CEFR level within it (Model B).** Demo both flows so the team sees the trade-off before committing.
